]]> A Guinness World Record silver bar refined and cast by Sam Precious Metals debuts at the Dubai Precious Metals Conference 2025.
 
Dubai, UAE – November 25, 2025:

Sam Precious Metals, one of the Middle East’s leading accredited precious-metals refineries, has produced the world’s largest silver bar – an extraordinary Guinness World Record-breaking creation weighing 1971 kgs, reflecting the UAE’s founding year and engraved with the map of the country. The bar was unveiled at the Dubai Precious Metals Conference 2025 (DPMC), where it immediately drew global attention as a landmark achievement for the region and a striking demonstration of Dubai’s expanding capabilities in regulated precious-metals innovation.

Refined, cast, and finished entirely within Sam’s state-of-the-art facilities, the bar embodies a level of technical mastery rarely required in conventional precious-metals production. Its creation demanded a specialized casting mold engineered to withstand the weight and temperature of molten silver on an unprecedented scale, as well as precisely controlled melting and cooling phases to prevent internal stress fractures. Maintaining purity at record-breaking volume required highly coordinated workflow across metallurgical, engineering, and safety teams, followed by rigorous structural inspections to ensure stability and surface integrity. The result is a singular feat of craftsmanship and engineering that sets a new benchmark for the global precious metals industry.

The project brought together contributions from select regional partners, with Sam Precious Metals leading the initiative both in the full physical production of the world-record bar and in funding its creation. Tokinvest introduced the digital infrastructure that will allow investors to access regulated fractional ownership of the asset, and DMCC served as the ecosystem’s regulatory and trade facilitator, reinforcing Dubai’s established role as a global hub for commodities and precious-metals activities.

Reflecting on the achievement, Mr. Sami Abu Ahmad, Chairman of Sam Precious Metals, stated: “Producing the world’s largest silver bar represents a defining moment for our refinery and for the region’s precious-metals sector. The technical challenge behind this achievement was substantial, requiring deep coordination between our engineering and refinery teams to ensure purity, stability, and structural precision at a scale rarely attempted. This project highlights what becomes possible when specialized partners contribute their expertise toward achieving a shared milestone. We are proud to contribute this achievement to the UAE’s growing reputation as a global center for precious-metals excellence.”

In addition to its historical and symbolic importance, the bar will also be made available as a regulated digital investment through Tokinvest, allowing qualified investors to hold fractional representations backed by Sam’s verified physical production. This initiative aligns with Sam’s broader strategy to strengthen global trust in responsibly refined precious metals, supported by the
refinery’s own internationally recognized certifications, including UAE Good Delivery, the Responsible Jewellery Council Chain of Custody (RJC CoC), amongst others, and full adherence to OECD-aligned sourcing practices.

About Sam Precious Metals

Sam Precious Metals is a leading fully accredited precious-metals refinery headquartered in Dubai, United Arab Emirates, specializing in the refining, assaying, casting, and minting of gold and silver to the highest international standards. The company holds UAE Good Delivery accreditation for both gold and silver, along with the Responsible Jewellery Council Chain of Custody (RJC CoC) and Code of Practices (RJC CoP) certification, amongst others, and maintains strict compliance with OECD-aligned responsible-sourcing frameworks. With advanced refining technology, specialized die-creation and minting capabilities, and a strong commitment to ethical business practices, Sam Precious Metals serves global clients across investment, jewelry, industrial, and bullion sectors. The refinery is recognized for its commitment to transparency, quality, and innovation, and continues to contribute to the UAE’s growing prominence as a global hub for precious-metals excellence.

Sam Precious Metals is proud to announce the signing of a strategic academic partnership between Sam Training Center and the École de Commerce de Lyon, one of Europe’s leading business schools recognized for its global academic footprint, innovation-focused curriculum, and international executive education programs. 

With this association, the Sam Training Center is now renamed to Sam Precious Metals Academy and holds full accreditation as a higher-education institution empowered to confer formally recognized academic degrees. This milestone transitions our academic platform beyond professional certification into a structured degree-granting model, supported by collaborative curriculum development, faculty exchange, credit pathways, and a globally connected academic structure.  

Students and faculty will benefit from coordinated academic programs delivered across 11 international branches of École de Commerce de Lyon, delivered both physically and virtually, enabling global access to structured, industry-aligned learning. 

This partnership reflects our long-standing commitment to academic rigor, ethical governance, and sector-specific specialization. The programs developed under this collaboration will foster a new generation of industry leaders equipped with the skills required to uphold transparency, compliance, sustainability, market integrity, and operational excellence within the global precious metals landscape. 

With this initiative, Sam strengthens its position as a catalyst for knowledge transfer and institutional development in our industry – building pathways that are not only educational, but foundational to a more responsible and professionally equipped global precious-metals ecosystem. 

Additional program information and formal academic timelines will be shared as the next phase is announced.

Sam Precious Metals is pleased to announce a significant milestone in the advancement of international education within the precious metals sector. 

Earlier this year in Paris, Mr. Sami Abu Ahmad formalized an academic cooperation agreement at the National Assembly of France alongside Mr. Hervé Diaz and Mr. Bruno Fuchs, President and Honorary President of the Circle of French-speaking Students of France. 

This cooperation marks a strategic step toward supporting étudiants francophones not only in France and UAE, but across Europe, Africa, and the Middle East – regions that hold historic importance in the global gold industry and account for French-speaking nations that include many of the world’s most prominent gold-producing markets. 

Through this partnership, French-speaking students will gain structured access to specialized academic and industry pathways, including training in responsible sourcing, compliance, refining sciences, market integrity, and sector-specific governance. 

This initiative aligns closely with the evolution of the Sam Training Center, now known as Sam Precious Metals Academy, which is transitioning into a broader academic body dedicated to elevating educational standards globally. As part of this development, new academic bridges are being established to further serve diverse international communities, including French-speaking markets critical to precious metals supply chains and economic growth. 

By extending these academic foundations to the Francophone world, Sam reaffirms its commitment to education as a tool for structural empowerment, ethical industry development, and long-term international cooperation. This agreement represents not only the strengthening of cultural and academic ties, but also the advancement of communities deeply connected to the global gold economy. 

Further academic and enrolment updates will be announced in due course. 

Renforcer les parcours académiques mondiaux pour les communautés francophones

Sam Precious Metals a le plaisir d’annoncer une étape majeure dans le développement de l’éducation internationale au sein du secteur des métaux précieux. 

Plus tôt cette année, à Paris, M. Sami Abu Ahmad a officialisé un accord de coopération académique à l’Assemblée nationale de France aux côtés de M. Hervé Diaz et M. Bruno Fuchs, respectivement Président et Président d’honneur du Cercle des Étudiants Francophones de France. 

Cette coopération marque une avancée stratégique pour soutenir les étudiants francophones, non seulement en France et aux Émirats arabes unis, mais également à travers l’Europe, l’Afrique et le Moyen-Orient — des régions qui occupent une place historique dans l’industrie mondiale de l’or et regroupent de nombreuses nations francophones parmi les plus grands marchés aurifères du monde. 

Grâce à ce partenariat, les étudiants francophones bénéficieront d’un accès structuré à des parcours académiques et professionnels spécialisés, incluant des formations en approvisionnement responsable, conformité, sciences du raffinage, intégrité du marché et gouvernance sectorielle. 

Cette initiative s’inscrit dans la continuité de l’évolution du Centre de Formation Sam, désormais connu sous le nom de Sam Precious Metals Academy, qui se transforme en un organisme académique plus vaste, dédié à l’élévation des standards éducatifs à l’échelle mondiale. Dans ce cadre, de nouveaux ponts académiques sont établis pour mieux servir les communautés internationales, notamment les marchés francophones essentiels aux chaînes d’approvisionnement et à la croissance économique des métaux précieux. 

En étendant ces fondations académiques au monde francophone, Sam réaffirme son engagement envers l’éducationcomme outil d’autonomisation structurelle, de développement éthique du secteur et de coopération internationale durable. Cet accord représente non seulement un renforcement des liens culturels et académiques, mais aussi un progrès concret pour les communautés profondément connectées à l’économie mondiale de l’or. 

Des mises à jour supplémentaires concernant les programmes académiques et les inscriptions seront annoncées prochainement. 

تعزيز المسارات الأكاديمية العالمية للمجتمعات الناطقة بالفرنسية

.يسر شركة سام للمعادن الثمينة أن تعلن عن إنجاز مهم في مسيرة تطوير التعليم الدولي ضمن قطاع المعادن الثمينة 

في وقت سابق من هذا العام في باريس، قام السيد سامي أبو أحمد بتوقيع اتفاقية تعاون أكاديمي في الجمعية الوطنية الفرنسية إلى جانب كل من السيد هيرفي دياز والسيد برونو فوكس، الرئيس والرئيس الفخري لـ دائرة الطلاب الناطقين بالفرنسية في فرنسا 

ويمثل هذا التعاون خطوة استراتيجية لدعم الطلاب الناطقين بالفرنسية، ليس فقط في فرنسا ودولة الإمارات العربية المتحدة، بل أيضًا في أوروبا وإفريقيا والشرق الأوسط — وهي مناطق ذات أهمية تاريخية في صناعة الذهب العالمية، وتضم عددًا كبيرًا من الدول الفرنكوفونية التي تُعد من أهم الأسواق المنتجة للذهب في العالم 

من خلال هذه الشراكة، سيتمكن الطلاب الناطقون بالفرنسية من الوصول إلى مسارات أكاديمية ومهنية متخصصة ومنظمة تشمل التدريب في مجالات مثل التوريد المسؤول، والامتثال، وعلوم التكرير، ونزاهة السوق، وحوكمة القطاع 

تأتي هذه المبادرة في إطار التطور الذي يشهده مركز التدريب التابع لشركة سام للمعادن الثمينة، والذي أصبح يُعرف الآن باسم أكاديمية سام للمعادن الثمينة، حيث يتم تحويله إلى مؤسسة أكاديمية موسّعة تهدف إلى رفع معايير التعليم على المستوى العالمي. وكجزء من هذا التطور، يتم إنشاء جسور أكاديمية جديدة لخدمة المجتمعات الدولية المتنوعة، بما في ذلك الأسواق الفرنكوفونية التي تلعب دورًا رئيسيًا في سلاسل توريد المعادن الثمينة والنمو الاقتصادي العالمي 

ومن خلال توسيع هذه القواعد الأكاديمية لتشمل العالم الناطق بالفرنسية، تؤكد شركة سام للمعادن الثمينة التزامها الراسخ بـ التعليم كأداة للتمكين الهيكلي، وتطوير الصناعة الأخلاقي والمسؤول، وتعزيز التعاون الدولي طويل الأمد 

ويمثل هذا الاتفاق تعزيزًا للعلاقات الثقافية والأكاديمية، وكذلك تقدمًا حقيقيًا للمجتمعات المرتبطة ارتباطًا وثيقًا باقتصاد الذهب العالمي
